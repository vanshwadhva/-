Autopilot & Supervised Learning

Moving beyond code to intelligence. Discover how our vehicles learn to navigate the world through billions of miles of real-world human driving data.

Foundation

Standard Autopilot

Our baseline driver assistance system. Standard Autopilot enables your car to steer, accelerate, and brake automatically within its lane. It uses a combination of deep vision and ultrasonic sensing to provide a safety layer that is always active.

  • ● Traffic-Aware Cruise Control: Matches speed to surrounding traffic.
  • ● Autosteer: Assists in steering within a clearly marked lane.
The Evolution

Svashasan Pilot (Supervised)

Where traditional software meets Artificial Intelligence. Unlike rule-based systems, Svashasan Pilot is powered by end-to-end neural networks trained on high-quality driving data from our entire fleet.

  • ● Neural Decision Making: The car doesn't follow "if-then" rules; it understands the intent of other road users.
  • ● Contextual Awareness: Better handling of complex intersections, construction zones, and unpredictable weather.

How It Learns

Supervised Learning is the process of training our AI models by showing them millions of examples of how the best human drivers react to specific scenarios.

01

Data Ingestion

Anonymous driving clips are uploaded from the fleet when the AI's prediction differs from the human driver's action.

02

Auto-Labeling

Our 4D labeling engine automatically identifies pedestrians, traffic lights, and road geometry across thousands of frames.

03

Training & Deployment

The model is retrained on our supercomputing cluster and pushed back to vehicles via Over-the-Air (OTA) updates.

The Importance of Supervision

While the AI is capable of handling most maneuvers, "Supervised" means the driver remains responsible for the vehicle at all times. Our cabin-facing camera and steering wheel sensors ensure the driver is attentive and ready to take over if the environment becomes too complex.

"Driving is a collaborative effort between human intuition and machine precision."
