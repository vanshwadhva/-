Introducing Svashasan FSD v12: The End-to-End Revolution

March 11, 2026

Visualization of the FSD v12 neural network processing complex urban navigation in real-time.

BENGALURU, India — Svashasan Labs is proud to announce the rollout of Full Self-Driving (FSD) v12, a fundamental shift in how our vehicles perceive and navigate the world.

For the past five years, autonomous driving has relied on complex, human-coded heuristics—thousands of lines of "if-this-then-that" logic. Today, we are retiring that approach. FSD v12 is powered by a single, unified end-to-end neural network that translates raw camera pixels directly into steering, braking, and acceleration commands.

From Code to Intuition

The breakthrough of v12 lies in its ability to learn from the best drivers in our fleet. By training on millions of miles of high-quality human driving data, the system has developed a "biological" intuition for the road. It no longer needs to be told how to handle a complex roundabout or a faded lane marking; it simply understands the context through pattern recognition.

"V12 isn't just an update; it's a rebirth. We've deleted over 300,000 lines of C++ code and replaced them with a neural network that is more capable, more resilient, and significantly smoother than anything we've built before." — Vikram Das, VP of Software Engineering

Powered by Param-1

The deployment of FSD v12 was made possible by our recently unveiled Param-1 supercomputing cluster. The massive compute power allowed our team to train models with billions of parameters, capturing the subtle nuances of human driving that traditional software could never replicate.

This transition drastically reduces latency. Because the entire driving stack resides within a single network, the time between seeing a hazard and taking action is now measured in milliseconds—faster than human reaction time.

The neural network architecture of v12 mimics the human visual cortex to prioritize safety-critical objects.

Safety First, Always

Initial testing of v12 shows a 45% reduction in "intervention-needed" events in dense urban traffic. The system handles "unprotected left turns" and "tight merging" with a level of confidence and smoothness that was previously unattainable.

As we continue to gather data and retrain our models on the Param-1 cluster, the system will only get better. We are one step closer to a world where transportation is not just autonomous, but safer for everyone on the road.
